I am a Ph.D. student in Economics at Bocconi University, with primary research interests in Decision Theory, Stochastic Choice, Microeconomic Theory, and Behavioural Economics.

Before starting my doctoral studies, I worked as a pre-doctoral researcher at the Free University of Bozen-Bolzano, where I contributed to a project on renewable energy and electricity markets from an industrial organization perspective.

My methodological training was shaped during my M.A. in Economics at Collegio Carlo Alberto in Turin, where I received rigorous preparation in economic theory and econometrics—foundations that proved invaluable throughout my research experience.

When I am not thinking about choice under uncertainty (which is less often than one might hope), I occasionally enjoy some free time. I usually spend it reading or watching TV series, and, when the weather cooperates, hiking.
